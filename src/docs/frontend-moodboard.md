# Forgotten Industries Front-End Moodboard

This is the working visual direction for the public domain site at `forgotten-industries.net`.

The site should feel like a technical archive, restoration log, evidence notebook, repair manual, artifact catalog, and evidence-based memoir. The archive is the art.

## Core Feel

- Recovered, not invented.
- Durable, not flashy.
- Technical, but still human.
- Editorial, but not magazine-glossy.
- Museum object record crossed with repair bench notes.
- 70 percent straight technical record, 30 percent poetic / artistic voltage.
- Classically trained scientist with field-radio rapport.
- Antique, yellowed, dusted, noisy, but clear.
- Impactful, altruistic, evidence-based, and optimistic despite the record.
- Naturally accessible: high-contrast first, not high-contrast as an afterthought.

## Inspiration Research

Use these as translation references, not as materials to imitate directly.

### Godspeed You! Black Emperor / Silver Mt. Zion / Efrim Menuck

Constellation's Godspeed notes emphasize longform instrumental work, protest music, analog 16mm film loops, tactile physical packaging, and a refusal to center personality, exposure, or commodification.

Design translation:

- Let the work speak.
- Favor analog tactility over digital gloss.
- Use slow accumulation: records, captions, source links, fragments, and evidence.
- Hold grief, resistance, and optimism without spectacle.
- Keep the site anti-celebrity and anti-feed.

The Silver Mt. Zion and Efrim layer adds human weather: rough notation, hand-built work, directness, stubbornness, fatigue, memory, and stubborn light.

Design translation:

- Permit poetic copy only when it sharpens the record.
- Use human language in short, controlled doses.
- Let imperfections remain visible when they are evidence.
- Keep warmth under the technical surface.

### Metal Gear Solid Codec / Mission Support

Konami's current manual describes Codec Mode as a way to contact people for support, hints, and information, with frequency selection, memory, saving, and mission log behavior.

Design translation:

- Treat interface labels like mission-control support, not roleplay.
- Use frequency, signal, source, memory, and mission-log language sparingly.
- Make status and next action easy to find.
- The site should feel like a calm voice in the wire: useful first, atmospheric second.

## Visual Anchors

- Real archive photos should lead the design.
- Hardware photos should stay inspectable: fittings, boards, cases, tubing, labels, dust, parts, and bench state.
- The Forgotten Industries mark should remain severe and industrial, but the surrounding system should be calmer and easier to read.
- Images are evidence, not decoration.
- Noise and age should appear as paper/scan texture, not as visual clutter.

## Palette

- Paper: `#02040a`
- Ink: `#fff9e8`
- Muted: `#d7d1ff`
- Line: `#4f8cff`
- Steel: `#84eaff`
- Oxide: `#ff5bd6`
- Brass: `#fff275`
- Blueprint: `#67e8ff`
- White / surface: `#081327`

Use a dark archive surface by default, with warm ink, cyan-blue links, magenta evidence labels, and yellow focus states. The reference point is old operating-system high contrast: blue, pink, yellow, and black, but calmer and more readable.

The background may carry a faint scan/grid texture, but `prefers-contrast: more` should strip the texture back to a plain black surface and strengthen contrast.

## Typography

- Serif for editorial identity and long-form reading.
- Sans for labels, navigation, metadata, and component headings.
- Monospace only for short machine-readable IDs, source notes, statuses, or file-like references.
- Keep letter spacing at `0`.
- Avoid over-stylizing the typography. The page should read like an archive, not a poster.
- Section rail headers should read like research-paper figure legends: `FIGURE 1`, compact ATLAS dispatch sentence, visually quieter than the evidence column.
- Stencil energy belongs in stamps and project marks, not body copy: `ON BENCH`, `RECOVERED`, `ENTRY 000`, `FIELD LOG`, `DOSSIER`.
- Fixed-width type should carry metadata and evidence structure, not the whole page.

## Component Grammar

Build from:

- ledgers
- artifact records
- source links
- shelf indexes
- field notes
- inventory rows
- restoration status blocks
- object photos with captions

Avoid turning everything into floating cards. Use full-width bands, crisp dividing lines, and constrained inner content.

## Copy Rules

Use words like:

- archive
- field notes
- restoration
- evidence
- artifact
- dossier
- source
- recovery
- machine
- preservation

Avoid:

- brand
- content
- journey
- hustle
- generic creator language
- vague inspirational sections
- direct imitation of any specific band package or game UI

## Practical Direction

The public site should open with a clear identity, real archive evidence, and routes into the current shelves. It should not look like a SaaS landing page or a portfolio template.
